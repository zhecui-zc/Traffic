SOTI Research
Experiment description:

Research cocreation between Traffic and precision of location.

This experiment is to explore the relationship between Rssi and precision of CMX reports. I set 25 test points every 4 meters in the office as ground truth (no test point 9). I requested the CMX API once per second to collect data, using two devices during the experiment, a Samsung phone (f4:02:28:5a:28:63) and a Raspberry pi(dc:a6:32:26:67:ab ). Each device collected 10 minutes of data at each test site, more than 500 records, and I collected 26,437 records of data in total. For test points 1 to 15, Samsung phone was connected to SOTI Guest Wi-Fi. From test point 16 to test point 26, Samsung phone was connected to SOTI RTLS Wi-Fi, and raspberry PI was connected to SOTI RTLS Wi-Fi throughout the experiment.

CMX definition of Rssi: If Rssi is 30, it means the device is in a square with sides of length 60feet, which is the vertical distance from the center of the square to each variable length is 30feet.

For evaluating the positioning performance of the CMX, I chose Root Mean Square Error (RMSE).

I made a multi-dimensional analysis of the data and drew a series of scatter diagrams. Firstly, the performance of different devices at different test points and the performance of different devices in the whole experiment were analyzed. Scatter charts were also drawn according to different test points to observe the performance of different devices at the same test point, and the comparison between unassociated Samsung phone and associated Samsung phone, as well as the comparison of experimental results of all test points with different devices.
